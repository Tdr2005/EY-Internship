Dal Bati

Dal bati is a popular Rajasthani dish that is a staple food in the state. It is a combination of two main components:
Dal: This is a spicy lentil stew, typically made with toor dal (pigeon peas) or moong dal (green gram). It is cooked with a blend of spices like cumin, coriander, chili powder, and turmeric.
Bati: These are hard, round wheat balls that are baked in an oven or on an open fire. They are made from a dough of wheat flour, ghee, and sometimes yogurt.
How it's eaten:
The bati is traditionally broken into pieces and dipped into the dal before being eaten. It can also be served with:
Churma: A sweet dish made by crushing the leftover bati and mixing it with sugar, ghee, and sometimes dry fruits.
Pickle: A tangy pickle, often mango or lemon, adds a refreshing flavor to the dish.
Salad: A simple salad of onions and tomatoes complements the spicy dal and the rich bati.
