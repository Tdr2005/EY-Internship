Samosa: A Crispy, Savory Delight

Samosa is a beloved snack or appetizer found throughout South Asia and the Middle East. It consists of a triangular pastry filled with savory ingredients and deep-fried to a golden crisp.
While the exact origins are debated, samosas are believed to have evolved from the sanbusaq, a baked dish mentioned in medieval Arabic cookbooks. Over time, the dish traveled across regions, leading to diverse variations in shape, filling, and cooking methods.
The most popular filling for samosas is a spiced mixture of potatoes and peas. However, other common fillings include:
Meat: Ground lamb, beef, or chicken
Vegetables: Onions, lentils, cauliflower, or paneer (Indian cottage cheese)
Spices: Cumin, coriander, chili powder, garam masala
